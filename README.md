 Education-Crisis-in-Kenya-From-Policy-to-Safety
 
This portfolio contains two end‑to‑end data analysis projects examining critical challenges in Kenya’s education sector. The first project evaluates the transition from the 8‑4‑4 system to the Competency‑Based Curriculum (CBC). The second project investigates the 2026 wave of secondary school unrest, including strikes, arson, and fatalities.
Together, they demonstrate my ability to:
- Define a real‑world problem and identify measurable indicators.
- Gather, clean, and structure data from reports, news, and official sources.
- Perform descriptive, diagnostic, and predictive analysis using spreadsheets, interactive dashboards, and Python.
- Translate findings into actionable policy recommendations.

- Project 1: CBC Transition in Kenya
- Goal: Identify which counties are most at risk of failing the CBC rollout and why.

- Key Findings
- - Non‑ASAL counties average 65.8% FLANA score vs ASAL counties 56.2% (gap of 9.6 points, p < 0.001).
- Teacher shortage severity explains 74% of the variance in learning outcomes (R² = 0.74).
- Over 150,000 students dropped out between Grade 3 and Grade 9 (11.9% of cohort).
- 14 counties are classified as “Critical Need” – they require 70% of new investment.

    Project 2: School Unrest in Kenya (2026)
    Goal: Understand the drivers of school fires, strikes, and walkouts, and identify high‑risk counties.

    Key Findings
    - Nakuru and Narok counties account for 42% of all affected schools (30 of 72).
- Counties with Infrastructure Grade D and Low fire safety compliance represent 76% of affected schools.
- Dormitory overcrowding correlates strongly with schools affected (r = 0.85).
- Five high‑risk counties (Nakuru, Narok, Taita Taveta, Machakos, Uasin Gishu) score above 80/100 on a composite risk scale.

    Why These Projects Matter
    Kenya’s education system is at a crossroads. The CBC promises to modernise learning, but underfunding, teacher shortages, and digital divides are leaving many counties behind. Simultaneously, the 2026 unrest crisis reveals deep infrastructural and safety failures in boarding schools. Both issues demand data‑driven, county‑specific solutions.

    My analysis provides:
- A clear ranking of county risk (CBC transition and school unrest).
- Statistical evidence linking resource gaps to poor outcomes.
- Interactive dashboards for policymakers to explore scenarios.
- Targeted recommendations for immediate action.
