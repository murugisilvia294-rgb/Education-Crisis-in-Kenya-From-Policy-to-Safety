# School Unrest in Kenya – Data Analytics Project

---

## Executive Summary

### Overview
This project analyzes school unrest in Kenya, including strikes, arson attacks, dormitory fires, and student fatalities. The objective is to understand key drivers of unrest and identify high-risk counties using data-driven methods to support education policy and decision-making.

---

### Problem Statement
Recent years have seen a rise in school unrest incidents across Kenya, raising concerns about student safety and school infrastructure. This project identifies patterns and key risk factors contributing to these incidents and translates them into actionable insights for intervention.

---

### Methodology

The analysis was conducted using a three-tier approach:

- **Google Sheets**
  - Data cleaning
  - Descriptive statistics
  - Exploratory data analysis

- **Power BI**
  - Interactive dashboards
  - Geographic mapping of risk
  - Root cause analysis and visualization

- **Python (Google Colab)**
  - Correlation analysis
  - Regression modeling
  - Clustering of counties
  - Hypothesis testing

A Composite Risk Score (0–100) was developed using:

- Infrastructure quality  
- Fire safety compliance  
- Dormitory overcrowding  

---

### Key Findings

- Nakuru and Narok are disproportionately affected by school unrest  
- Poor infrastructure and weak fire safety compliance strongly correlate with higher incidents  
- Overcrowding is a major driver of unrest in boarding schools  
- A small number of counties account for the majority of high-risk cases  

---

### Conclusion
School unrest is strongly linked to infrastructure and resource constraints. Data-driven risk scoring provides a structured way to identify vulnerable counties and prioritize interventions to improve school safety.

---

### Recommendations

- Conduct urgent fire safety audits in high-risk schools  
- Reduce dormitory overcrowding to safe capacity levels  
- Improve infrastructure in under-resourced schools  
- Implement a national monitoring system for school safety trends
