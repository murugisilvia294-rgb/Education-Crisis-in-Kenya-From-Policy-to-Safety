# CBC Transition Readiness in Kenya – Data Analytics Project

## Project Overview

Kenya's transition to the Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) has created new demands on school infrastructure, learning resources, and staffing. This project analyzes school readiness indicators to assess whether institutions are adequately prepared for the CBC transition and to identify areas requiring intervention.

The analysis focuses on infrastructure capacity, teacher availability, learning resources, and other readiness metrics to support evidence-based decision-making in the education sector.

---

## Objectives

* Assess school readiness for CBC implementation
* Identify infrastructure and resource gaps
* Evaluate teacher availability and capacity
* Highlight counties or schools requiring urgent intervention
* Provide actionable recommendations for policymakers and education stakeholders

---

## Key Questions

* Are schools adequately prepared for the CBC transition?
* Which readiness indicators have the largest gaps?
* How does readiness vary across counties or regions?
* What factors are most strongly associated with successful CBC implementation?

## Methodology

### Data Collection

School-level and county-level readiness indicators were collected from publicly available education datasets and reports.

### Data Cleaning

* Standardized data formats
* Removed duplicates
* Validated missing values
* Corrected inconsistencies in school and county names

### Data Analysis

* Descriptive statistics
* Readiness scoring
* Comparative analysis
* Correlation analysis
* Dashboard development

### Data Visualization

Interactive dashboards were developed to communicate findings and support stakeholder decision-making.

---

## Key Findings

* Infrastructure readiness varies significantly across regions.
* Teacher shortages remain a challenge in several areas.
* Some schools face capacity constraints that may affect CBC implementation.
* Resource availability differs considerably between counties.

---

## Recommendations

* Prioritize infrastructure investments in underprepared schools.
* Recruit and deploy additional teachers where shortages exist.
* Increase access to CBC learning resources and facilities.
* Establish continuous monitoring of readiness indicators.
* Support county-specific interventions based on identified gaps.

## Limitations

* Findings depend on the quality and completeness of available data.
* Some readiness indicators may not capture all aspects of CBC implementation.
* The analysis identifies patterns and gaps but does not establish causation.
